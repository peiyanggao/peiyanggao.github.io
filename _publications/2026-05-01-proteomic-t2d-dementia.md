This study leveraged the UK Biobank Pharma Proteomics Project (UKB-PPP) to investigate proteomic signatures distinguishing dementia risk between type 2 diabetes (T2D) patients and non-T2D individuals, with a median follow-up of 14.6 years across 52,958 participants (including 3,292 with T2D).

**Key findings:**

- Cox regression models with interaction terms identified 471 proteins whose associations with dementia risk differed significantly by T2D status. NPTXR (HR=0.74) and CA14 (HR=0.67) were protective only in non-T2D individuals, while ARHGEF12 (HR=1.45) increased dementia risk specifically in T2D patients.
- A LASSO-based 51-protein model achieved excellent prediction of 15-year dementia risk in T2D patients (AUC=0.835, C-index=0.829), outperforming traditional clinical risk scores and maintaining high accuracy for both Alzheimer's disease and vascular dementia subtypes.
- Plasma proteomic profiles revealed distinct interaction patterns for insulin (164 proteins) and biguanides (90 proteins), suggesting medication-specific molecular pathways modulating dementia risk in T2D.
- Weighted gene co-expression network analysis identified IL6-JAK-STAT3 signaling and epithelial-mesenchymal transition as common mechanisms linking T2D to dementia, while fatty acid metabolism dysregulation was specific to T2D-associated Alzheimer's disease.

This work provides one of the first comprehensive proteomic frameworks for dementia risk stratification in T2D populations, identifies actionable molecular targets for early intervention, and elucidates biological pathways for therapeutic development.
