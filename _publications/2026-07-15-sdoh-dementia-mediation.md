This study leveraged the UK Biobank cohort (359,419 participants, median follow-up 13.46 years) to investigate how social determinants of health (SDOH) contribute to dementia risk, and which mental health, biological aging, and lifestyle pathways mediate this relationship.

**Key findings:**

- Participants with favorable social conditions showed substantially reduced dementia risk compared to those with unfavorable social circumstances.
- Mediation analyses identified depression, frailty, biological aging (PhenoAge), and smoking as key intermediate pathways, mediating 24.00%, 13.60%, 6.33%, and 2.82% of the SDOH-dementia association, respectively.
- Together, these pathways account for a substantial share of the excess dementia risk linked to unfavorable social determinants.

This work highlights mental health, biological aging, and lifestyle as modifiable, actionable targets through which social interventions could help reduce dementia risk driven by social determinants of health.
