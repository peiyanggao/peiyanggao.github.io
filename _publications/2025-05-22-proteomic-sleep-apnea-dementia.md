This study leveraged the UK Biobank cohort to investigate plasma proteomic signatures predicting dementia risk in patients with sleep apnea, a condition affecting one-seventh of adults globally and strongly associated with cognitive decline.

**Key findings:**

- Sleep apnea was significantly associated with increased risk of all-cause dementia, Alzheimer's disease, and vascular dementia in 328,411 participants, with stronger associations observed in individuals with BMI > 25 kg/m².
- Among 1,282 sleep apnea patients with proteomic data (median follow-up 13.58 years), 29 proteins were significantly associated with all-cause dementia, 24 with Alzheimer's disease, and 2 with vascular dementia after FDR correction.
- GFAP showed the strongest predictive performance for all-cause dementia (C-index = 0.708), reflecting hypoxia-associated astrocyte activation and neuroinflammation.
- DIPK2B emerged as the top predictor for Alzheimer's disease (C-index = 0.788), and KLK3 (kallikrein-related peptidase 3) for vascular dementia (C-index = 0.732), with both showing sleep apnea-specific associations not observed in non-apnea individuals.
- Combining KLK3 with clinical variables substantially improved vascular dementia prediction (AUC = 0.853), highlighting potential clinical utility.

This work identifies the first plasma biomarker panel specifically tailored for dementia risk stratification in sleep apnea populations, suggesting novel mechanisms involving the kallikrein-kinin system and offering new opportunities for early intervention of cognitive decline in sleep apnea patients.
