This study leveraged the UK Biobank cohort to perform a comprehensive longitudinal proteomic analysis of incident epilepsy, examining 2,920 plasma proteins across 52,372 participants with 440 incident epilepsy cases over a mean follow-up of 13.9 years.

**Key findings:**

- 103 plasma proteins showed significant associations with incident epilepsy after Bonferroni correction, with neurofilament light polypeptide (NEFL, HR = 2.13) and growth differentiation factor 15 (GDF15, HR = 1.82) exhibiting the strongest correlations.
- Temporal trajectory analyses revealed that abnormal changes in epilepsy-associated protein levels began up to 15 years before clinical diagnosis, with NEFL and GDF15 abnormalities detectable 14.2 and 11.2 years prior, respectively.
- Pathway and network analyses highlighted the immune response as a pivotal mechanism, identifying TNFRSF1A, CD274, HAVCR2, and TIMP1 as central hub proteins.
- The 103 epilepsy-associated proteins showed stronger correlations with stress-related life events than with genetic predisposition, with mediation analyses suggesting a partial role in linking life stress to epilepsy onset.
- A machine learning model based on the protein panel achieved robust prediction of future epilepsy (AUC = 0.789 for cases occurring >10 years after baseline), with 17 of these proteins identified as drug targets.

This work provides the largest plasma proteomic analysis of epilepsy to date, offering new insights into pre-clinical disease progression, immune-mediated pathogenesis, and potential biomarkers for early risk stratification and therapeutic development.
